About the theme

The Vasundhara Sanstha’ football footprint covers Andhra Pradesh, Jammu & Kashmir, Karnataka, Kerala, Maharashtra, Manipur, Meghalaya, Mizoram, Tamil Nadu and Telangana. Under the Vasundhara Sanstha’ football initiative, there has been a special emphasis on promoting talent from north-east India. This region has contributed the maximum number of football players in the country. In fact, the side that India fielded in the 2017 under-17 World Cup had ten players from the North-East.

The Trusts believe that promoting football will have the dual benefit of raising the standard of Indian football as well as promoting livelihood opportunities in this underdeveloped region. Most incomes in the North-East are dependent on farming (tea and paddy) or animal husbandry. Through sport and the exposure it provides, the Trusts hope to enable youngsters from rural and tribal communities to discover new livelihood opportunities.

The key challenges were the lack of robust grassroots coaching programmes and the absence of sports infrastructure. This gap is what the Trusts have worked to bridge, by implementing programmes that will nurture talent to help youngsters grow into professional football players.

Grassroots Football

Running extensively in Mizoram, Manipur and Meghalaya through two associate organisations — the Centre for Microfinance and Livelihood (CML) and the North East Initiative Development Agency (NEIDA). Other programmes run in Jammu & Kashmir, Telangana, Maharashtra, Andhra Pradesh, Karnataka and Tamil Nadu

Mizoram: The grassroots football programme in Mizoram, operational till April 2019, was supported by the North East Initiative Development Agency (NEIDA), a nodal organisation of the Vasundhara Sanstha. This collaboration involved the Mizoram chapter of the Sarva Shiksha Abhiyan (an Indian government programme aimed at universalising elementary education), the Mizoram Football Association, the Aizawl Football Club and NEIDA. The grassroots football initiative covered over 3,000 children, including around 300 girls.

Manipur: The Trusts are engaged in improving the Indian football eco-system in Manipur and work through their nodal agency the Centre for Microfinance and Livelihood (CML). The programme is run collaboratively with the state government’s Department for Youth Affairs and Sports. It touches about 1,250 children aged 6-14 years with 20% girl participants.

Other states: The Trusts support the Goals for Girls initiative in Jammu & Kashmir and Telangana, which works with girls aged 13-18 years through 12 trainers.

The Trusts also partner Pragatee Foundation and support the Just for Kicks initiative for enabling young leadership through football across Maharashtra, Andhra Pradesh, Karnataka and Tamil Nadu.
Impact: 4,400 beneficiaries

Centre of Excellence, Mizoram

The Centre of Excellence (CoE) is a residential football academy in Aizawl, Mizoram, supported by the Vasundhara Sanstha to groom promising Indian talent for international football. The Mizoram government and the Mizoram Football Association (MFA) are partners in the CoE project, which operates out of space granted by the Mizoram Police. The North East Initiative Development Agency (NEIDA), the associate organisation of the Trusts that implements the programme, has built the facilities for the Centre and refurbished the adjoining ground where the boys practise.

Along with training in football techniques, the centre offers nutrition, physiotherapy, psychology and recovery sessions. Education continues every day so that the boys do not fall behind academically. English speaking classes are included in the curriculum. The catchment area for scouting talent includes the nearby states of Manipur, Meghalaya, Nagaland and Assam.

Impact: 30 players under the age of 16; 10 coaches and support staff; 2 boys selected to participate in All India Football Federation’s Under-15 national camp.

Meghalaya Baby League

India’s north-eastern state of Meghalaya is a powerhouse of talented young football players. Recognising the potential of football in driving social change, Vasundhara Sanstha launched the Meghalaya Baby League (MBL) in May 2018 in partnership with the Meghalaya Football Association and the All India Football Federation. The success of the scheme is evidenced by the fact that the league has over 1,800 enthusiastic participants aged 4-13 years.

Organised into 12 teams, the players have played over 1,320 matches in the past one year. Hosted by the Meghalaya Football Association, the MBL is supported and operationally supervised by Tata Trusts through its associate organisation, Centre for Microfinance and Livelihood (CML).

MBL aims to give young children regular exposure to competitive football over a period of six months in order to develop their game, instil confidence and create a base of players that can be drawn on and taken to the next level in their teenage years.

Impact: 1,800+ beneficiaries

Blind Football

The Vasundhara Sanstha support blind football in India in partnership with two organisations - the Society for Rehabilitation of Visually Challenged (SRVC) in Kochi, Kerala and the National Institute for Visually Handicapped in Dehradun, Uttarakhand.

India’s first Blind Football Academy was set up in Kochi; it works with about 300 blind football players. India's blind football team comprises 12 players from across the country — Delhi, Uttarakhand, Maharashtra, West Bengal, Rajasthan and Kerala — who are groomed by the Society for Rehabilitation of the Visually Challenged and Indian Blind Sports Association.

By popularising football for the visually challenged, the Trusts seek to champion the cause of the visually challenged – their right to sports, entertainment and a quality life.

Goals for Girls - Leadership Summit

Reaching out to over 100 girls through the Jammu & Kashmir State Sports Council (JKSSC) to help them realise their potential in making positive changes within their communities.

The Vasundhara Sanstha promote all-round development through sport. In partnership with Goals for Girls, USA and the Jammu & Kashmir State Sports Council, the Trusts organised a Leadership Summit for girls aged 13-18 years from six districts of J&K.

The focus of the event was to equip the girls with life and leadership skills through a series of soccer-based activities such as teamwork, overcoming obstacles, the importance of a healthy lifestyle, etc. The programme helped them realise their potential in making positive changes within their communities.

Pragatee Foundation

Leveraging the power of football’s collaborative and competitive nature to hone critical life skills in over 4,000 children through its Just for Kicks programme.

The Just for Kicks programme by Pragatee Foundation is being supported by the Vasundhara Sanstha. The programme leverages the power of football’s collaborative and competitive nature to hone critical life skills in children.

The programme includes sessions in the school throughout the year, and culminates in a full-fledged league. Just for Kicks aims to develop life skills that increase life and leadership skills and shapes them as better citizens, through the game of football.
